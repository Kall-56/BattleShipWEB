Basic Rules

  1. Objective:

    Fully sink the enemy fleet before your opponent sinks yours.

  2. Game Components:

    The player's own board, where the placement of the ships by the player is shown, and another grid that represents the space where the opponent distributed his ships, where the failed or successful shots will be shown as the game progresses. Game board for each player (10x10 grids).

    Fleet of Ships:

    • 1 Carrier (5 cells)
    • 1 BattleShip (4 cells)
    • 1 Cruiser (3 cells)
    • 1 Submarine (3 cells)
    • 1 Destroyer (2 cells)

  3. Preparation:

    • Each player places his ships on the board, hidden from the opponent.
    • The ships can be positioned vertically or horizontally.
    • Ships cannot overlap.

  4. Turns:

    • Players take turns “shooting” at the opponent's trying to guess the position of the enemy ships.
    • Each shot is indicated by selecting a cell on the opponent's board.
    • The player whose turn it will be to “shoot” for the first time (first turn) will be selected at random.

  5. Turn Duration:

    There is a time limit for each turn of 60 seconds to keep the pace of the game. If a player does not make his move within the time limit, it is considered a lost turn.

  6. Fleet Impacts:

    • If a player hits a shot in a cell containing part of an enemy ship, a “hit” is marked.
    • If the shot does not hit any ship, a “miss” is marked.

  7. Fleet Power-Ups

    This game adds the ability to use power-ups, which allow you to use new mechanics against your opponent.

    1. Sonar:

      • Reveals a random cell of an enemy ship.
      • Available as long as the submarine has not been sunk.

    2. Attack Aircraft:

      • Launches up to 5 missiles at random on the enemy board.
      • Available as long as the carrier has not been sunk.

    3. Sea Mine:

      • Players can plant a mine on their own board.
      • If the enemy attacks a mine, them receives an additional “hit” on a random adjacent cell.

    4. Defensive Shield:

      • Protects a region of 3x3 cells for 3 turns.
      • Attacks in the protected area are nullified.
      • One use only per game.

    5. Cruise Missile:

      • Attack directed to an area of 3x3 cells.
      • Recharge time: 5 turns.

    6. Quick Repair:

      • Restores up to 2 cells of a damaged ship (not sunk).
      • Only one use per ship.

    7. EMP Attack:

      • Disables all special functions of the enemy for 3 turns.
      • Recharge time: 10 turns.

  8. Fleet Sinking:

    When all the cells of a ship have been hit, the ship is considered "sunk". The opponent is informed which ship has been sunk.

  9. Win Condition:

    The first player to sink the entire enemy fleet wins the game.
